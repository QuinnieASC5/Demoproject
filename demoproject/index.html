African American Timelime

1619

In 1619, the first batch of slaves were brought to the Thirteen Colonies. By the 1690 every colony had slaves.

1739

1739 was the first and biggest slave rebellion which orruced in South Carolina also known as the Stono Rebellion. This event shows a significance that African Americans still had hope and faith they survive slavery was at.

1808 - 1832

Congress banned importation for slaves.

In Boston, a man named William Lloyd Garrison started an anti-slavery news paper called the Liberator.

1831

Approximately 75,000 slaves escape to the North using the Underground Railroad and in 1847 a newpapers called the New Star was create by Ex-slave Frederick Douglass.

1849

Harriet Tubman escapes from slavery and becomes an instrumental leader of the Underground Railroad.

1860

Abraham Lincoln is elected president, angering the southern states.
